Utility poles have a number of options in terms of design, but most if not all are categorized based on their function and the wires they hoist. In the U.S., they are all, however, comprised of wooden poles and attachment pieces to hold wires. As these poles carry power from power substations to specific buildings and houses, many also have transformers in order to step down the high voltages of the main power lines to the lower voltages necessary for a respective building.
